Social Media and
Mistakes

Social media is now an ingrained part of our lives. Right? If you deleted all your social media, you would get lost in the world. Right? Well, some dissagree.

ðŸšªPart 1: Inception

At the beginning of the year, one of my goals was to keep off of social media for one day a month. I felt that it was controlling me, which is the main issue most have with it today. Utility is proven, instagram and youtube haveuses, but it's the feeling of uncontrollability that we have which makes most uneasy.

So, I had this as one of my goals for the first few months of the year, and I felt like it was working. I had less of a constant desire to look at social media, and the way you act outside of school/working, can effect how you act during it.

ðŸ”™Part 2: The Change

One of my pillar groups, or main groups of goals is relating to stoicism and tranquility, and this was one of few goal outcomes I had relating to it. I could clearly see if I was making progress towards the quite abstract wish of being more "stoic." And, around March I decided that since my relationship with social media was not uncontrollable, and That I didn't need any more detoxes.

I felt this was all fine and dandy until I came across a podcast which had been on my list for a while, one with Cal Newport on the Rich Roll Podcast (which I talk about on my email newsletter that you can sub to by clicking here!).

ðŸ¤”Part 3: Today

Newport presents a lot of reasons for why social media is in some ways optional, and how using it less betters you in general. From deeper work, to greater control of your life, it's all good.

How am I applying this to my own life? I'm setting an ambitious goal of not using social media (mostly not using youtube) for one day each week, which was my original goal to build up to. If this seems interesting to you at all, I highly suggest you try at least once a social media declutter, it might feel terrible, but as you do it more and more, you get off the initial withdrawl, for lack of a better word.
